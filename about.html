About the Workshop

Toward Human-Level Robotic Teammates: R&D State of the Art and Roadmap to the Future

Detailed Description

Developing robots that can operate as full-fledged members of human-robot teams is a core objective of R&D in robotics today. The emergence of agentic AI and the growing influence of neurosymbolic AI make it incumbent on the cognitive robotics community to assess their current and potential impact on issues of integrating intentionality and reasoning with embodiment and perception. While advances in machine learning-based, data-driven approaches have expanded the capabilities of robots in perception and control, cognitive modeling relying on symbolic knowledge, commonsense reasoning and ontologically-grounded perception interpretation is widely considered as the necessary complement to the empirical approaches for developing robust, intentional, reflective, trustworthy, explainable, and socially aware robots with whom their human teammates are able to interact as they would with other humans.

This workshop will bring together experts and students of cognitive robotics, agentic and neurosymbolic AI, and adjacent fields, such as cognitive systems and architectures, embodied intelligence, human-robot interaction (HRI), and multi-robot collaboration to explore a variety of approaches to developing robots that approach human levels of understanding, decision-making and intentional learning while maintaining optimal control, data-driven and reinforcement learning and execution capabilities.

The scope of the workshop is comprehensive – topics of interest include cognitive robotic architecture and comprehensive system development as well as topics related to the components of robotic systems – perception (visual, language, haptic, interoceptive, etc.), meaningful ontology-based interpretation of perception results, attention, scheduling, reasoning, decision-making (including theory of mind and metacognition), communicating conceptual decisions to robotic control, skill-based (reflexive) execution, memory organization, and interaction protocols between the cognitive and the robotic layers of the cognitive robot. We aim to foster dialogue on how to scale up from task-specific skills to integrated cognitive agency.
