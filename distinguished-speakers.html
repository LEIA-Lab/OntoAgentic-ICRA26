Distinguished Speakers

Leading researchers and practitioners in OntoAgentic robotics

Keynote Speakers

We are honored to host distinguished researchers and practitioners who are at the forefront of OntoAgentic robotics research and development.

Speaker 1

Professor of Computer Science, MIT

Dr. Smith is a leading researcher in ontological reasoning and multi-agent systems. Her work focuses on developing formal frameworks for knowledge representation in robotic systems and has been published in top-tier venues including IJCAI, AAAI, and ICRA. She has received numerous awards for her contributions to the field, including the ACM SIGAI Outstanding Contribution Award.

Keynote Talk: "Foundations of OntoAgentic Robotics"

This talk will explore the theoretical foundations of OntoAgentic robotics, examining how ontological reasoning can be integrated with agentic behavior to create more intelligent and adaptive robotic systems. We will discuss current challenges and future research directions in this emerging field, including novel approaches to knowledge representation, reasoning under uncertainty, and multi-agent coordination.

09:30 - 10:30 60 minutes
Website Google Scholar LinkedIn

Speaker 2

Senior Research Scientist, Google DeepMind

Dr. Johnson specializes in embodied AI and human-robot interaction. His research combines machine learning with robotic systems to enable more natural and effective collaboration between humans and robots in various application domains. He has led several breakthrough projects in robotic learning and has published extensively in top-tier conferences and journals.

Keynote Talk: "Future Directions in OntoAgentic Robotics"

This presentation will look ahead to the future of OntoAgentic robotics, discussing emerging trends, technological challenges, and potential applications. We will explore how advances in AI, robotics, and knowledge representation are converging to create new possibilities for intelligent robotic systems that can reason, learn, and adapt in complex environments.

16:00 - 17:00 60 minutes
Website Google Scholar LinkedIn
