Call for Contributions

We invite researchers, practitioners, and students to submit their work to the ICRA 2026 Workshop on Human-Level Robotic Teammates. We welcome contributions that advance the state of the art in cognitive robotics, agentic AI, neurosymbolic integration, and human-robot teaming.

Workshop Format

The workshop will be structured to encourage active discussion of core topics. While time will be allocated to research paper and poster presentations, the "main events" of the workshop will be two discussion panels:

  • Assessment Panel: Devoted to assessment of the strengths and weaknesses of current approaches to cognitive robotics
  • Community-Building Panel: Devoted to sketching a program of future work in the area

We do not intend to have invited talks because we want to stress the interactive nature of the workshop and encourage community dialogue.

Submission Types

Full Papers

6-8 pages including references. Original research contributions with novel theoretical or experimental results.

  • Novel algorithms or frameworks
  • Experimental validation
  • Comprehensive literature review

Extended Abstracts

2-4 pages including references. Work-in-progress, preliminary results, or position papers.

  • Early-stage research
  • Position statements
  • Preliminary experiments

Demo Proposals

1-2 pages describing interactive demonstrations or system prototypes.

  • Live system demonstrations
  • Interactive prototypes
  • Software tools

Topics of Interest

Cognitive Agent Architectures

  • Cognitive agents as high-level controllers
  • Orchestration of heterogeneous AI modules
  • Integration of perception, reasoning, planning, and action
  • Architectural patterns for cognitive robotics

Neurosymbolic Integration

  • Symbol grounding and linking
  • Hybrid reasoning approaches
  • Knowledge-based inference integration
  • Perceptual data to symbolic representations

Human-Robot Teaming

  • Theory of mind and mindreading
  • Role allocation and adaptive collaboration
  • Socially aware interaction strategies
  • Mixed human-robot team coordination

Trust & Explainability

  • Transparent reasoning mechanisms
  • Communicable decision-making
  • Self-explanation and accountability
  • Trustworthy cognitive architectures

Submission Guidelines

Format Requirements

  • Use the IEEE ICRA conference template
  • Submit as PDF format
  • Include all figures and tables
  • Ensure proper citation format

Review Process

  • Double-blind review process
  • Each submission reviewed by 2-3 reviewers
  • Reviews focus on novelty, technical quality, and relevance
  • Authors will receive detailed feedback

Presentation Format

  • Accepted papers: 10-12 minute presentation + 3-5 minute Q&A
  • Extended abstracts: 5-7 minute presentation + 2-3 minute Q&A
  • Demos: 5-10 minute live demonstration

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I submit work that has been published elsewhere?

We welcome submissions of work that has been published in other venues, as long as it is relevant to the workshop theme and you have permission to present it.

What is the page limit for extended abstracts?

Extended abstracts should be 2-4 pages including references, using the IEEE ICRA template.

Will the workshop proceedings be published?

Yes, accepted papers and extended abstracts will be included in the workshop proceedings, which will be made available online.

Can I present remotely if I cannot attend in person?

We encourage in-person attendance, but remote presentation options may be available for exceptional circumstances. Please contact the organizers for more information.
