Toward Human-Level Robotic Teammates R&D State of the Art and Roadmap to the Future

Date: TBD 2026
Time: 09:00 - 17:00
Location: TBD

Abstract

Developing robots that can operate as full-fledged members of human-robot teams is a core objective of R&D in robotics today. The emergence of agentic AI and the growing influence of neurosymbolic AI make it incumbent on the cognitive robotics community to assess their current and potential impact on issues of integrating intentionality and reasoning with embodiment and perception.

While advances in machine learning-based, data-driven approaches have expanded the capabilities of robots in perception and control, cognitive modeling relying on symbolic knowledge, commonsense reasoning and ontologically-grounded perception interpretation is widely considered as the necessary complement to the empirical approaches for developing robust, intentional, reflective, trustworthy, explainable, and socially aware robots with whom their human teammates are able to interact as they would with other humans.

This workshop will bring together experts and students of cognitive robotics, agentic and neurosymbolic AI and adjacent fields to explore a variety of approaches to developing robots that approach human levels of understanding, decision-making and intentional learning while maintaining optimal control, data-driven and reinforcement learning and execution capabilities. We aim to foster dialogue on how to scale up from task-specific skills to integrated cognitive agency.

Topics of Discussion

Cognitive Agent Architectures

Cognitive agents as high-level controllers for robotics, orchestration of heterogeneous AI modules, and integration of perception, reasoning, planning, and action execution

Neurosymbolic Integration

Symbol grounding linking perceptual data to symbolic representations and hybrid reasoning combining learned models with knowledge-based inference

Long-Horizon Planning

Planning under uncertainty with commonsense knowledge, multi-step coordination in real-world scenarios, and bridging short-term control with long-term goals

Human-Robot Teaming

Modeling others' beliefs, goals, and intentions, role allocation in mixed teams, and socially aware interaction strategies

Perception & Action Grounding

Mapping sensorimotor signals to symbolic structures, natural language grounding, and situated understanding of environments and objects

Knowledge Resources

Ontologies, lexicons, and world models for robotics, introspection and self-modeling, and knowledge repair mechanisms

Trust & Explainability

Transparent reasoning and communicable decision-making, self-explanation mechanisms, and designing trustworthy cognitive architectures

Learning & Adaptation

Intentional learning approaches, skill acquisition from human demonstrations, and continuous adaptation in dynamic environments
